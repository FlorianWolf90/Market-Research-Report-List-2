<p><p>The substation automation market is divided into two primary types: transmission substations and distribution substations. Transmission substations are crucial for high-voltage power transfer over long distances, emphasizing efficiency, reliability, and remote monitoring. In contrast, distribution substations operate at lower voltages, focusing on the local distribution of electricity to end-users. Both markets prioritize advanced automation technologies, enabling real-time data collection, enhanced system management, and improved operational efficiency, which are essential for modern energy demands and grid stability.</p></p>
